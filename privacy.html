Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 24, 2026

Lectern AI is a Mac app that runs on your computer. It does not phone home. It does not have servers. It does not collect analytics. The sections below describe this in detail so you can verify those claims against the app's App Store Connect privacy disclosures and the app's PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy manifest, which publishes the same information to Apple in machine-readable form.

1. Who we are

Lectern AI is developed and published by Ian Trimble, operating as Remnant Security. Contact: privacy@iantrimble.com. If you need a mailing address for regulatory correspondence, email us first and we will provide it.

2. Data Lectern AI itself collects

None that leaves your Mac. Specifically:

  • Your slides and presentations are read from the file(s) you select, processed locally in the app's sandbox, and written to the output folder you choose. No copy is kept by Lectern.
  • Generated talk tracks, handouts, labs, quizzes, diagrams, and PDFs are written to disk in the location you pick. Nothing is uploaded anywhere by Lectern.
  • Preferences (your chosen LLM provider, theme, output toggles, etc.) are stored in macOS UserDefaults inside the app's sandbox container.
  • API keys are stored in the macOS Keychain (encrypted) and cached in UserDefaults for fast access. They never leave your Mac except when used to authenticate requests to the LLM provider you chose, below.

Lectern ships with no telemetry, analytics SDK, crash reporter, or feature-flag service. No data is sent to Remnant Security or Ian Trimble in the course of using the app.

3. Data sent to third-party LLM providers (BYOK disclosure)

To generate talk tracks, handouts, quizzes, and other artifacts, Lectern needs to send the text of your slides to a large language model. You bring your own API key for one of seven supported providers. When you trigger a generation:

  1. Lectern reads the slide text + any bullets, notes, and your selected options.
  2. It authenticates directly to the provider using your API key — Remnant Security has no servers in this path and cannot see your content or your key.
  3. The provider processes the request under their privacy policy and terms of service.
  4. The response is returned to your Mac and written to the output folder.

Data governed by each provider's own privacy policy when you choose to use them:

  • Anthropic (Claude): anthropic.com/legal/privacy
  • Google (Gemini): policies.google.com/privacy
  • DeepSeek: chat.deepseek.com/downloads/DeepSeek Privacy Policy
  • Moonshot (Kimi): kimi.moonshot.cn — privacy policy
  • Alibaba (Qwen): terms.alicdn.com — privacy policy
  • xAI (Grok): x.ai/legal/privacy-policy
  • MiniMax: intl.minimaxi.com/protocol/privacy-policy

You are free to change provider per deck, or to disable AI generation entirely by simply not providing an API key — Lectern still supports importing your own talk tracks as plain text.

4. Live research (optional)

When “Include live research” is enabled in Output Options, Lectern queries one or more web-search providers (Brave Search, Serper.dev, or Tavily) using your API key for that provider. Search queries derived from your slide content are sent. The search results are passed to your chosen LLM and returned in the talk track with source citations. The same disclosure rules in Section 3 apply.

  • Brave Search: brave.com/privacy
  • Serper.dev: serper.dev/privacy
  • Tavily: tavily.com/privacy

5. API-key storage

Keys you enter are stored in the macOS Keychain (encrypted at rest by the OS) and cached in UserDefaults for fast access without repeatedly prompting for Keychain permission. Neither location leaves your Mac. You can delete a key at any time by clearing it from the app's Configuration screen, or by removing its entry from Keychain Access.app.

6. Subscription billing (Pro tier)

Pro subscriptions are processed by Apple StoreKit 2 on your Mac. Lectern never sees payment information — only a StoreKit receipt indicating your subscription state. Apple's privacy policy governs payment: apple.com/legal/privacy. Auto-renewal can be managed in App Store → your account → Subscriptions, per Apple's standard terms.

7. Children

Lectern includes a “K–12 education” subject template but the app itself is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from them. The app's LLM calls (Section 3) are subject to the respective provider's minimum-age rules; most require users to be 13 or older.

8. Cookies and tracking

This website (iantrimble.com) uses no cookies, no analytics, no tracking pixels. The app itself uses no cookies, no analytics, and no tracking pixels.

9. Data-rights requests

Because Remnant Security does not collect or store your data, there is no data we can correct, export, or delete on your behalf. Deletion of data held by third-party LLM providers should be requested directly from those providers, whose privacy policies are linked in Section 3.

10. Changes to this policy

If we materially change this policy we will update the “Last updated” date at the top and announce the change in the app's release notes. Minor wording changes may be made without a release notice.
